Clickjacking Attack on Login Page

Clickjacking is a type of web attack where an attacker tricks a user into clicking on a button or link on a webpage without the user's knowledge. This can be done by overlaying an invisible element on top of a legitimate button or link, making it seem like the user is clicking on the real thing when in fact they are interacting with the attacker's hidden element.

One common target for clickjacking attacks is login pages. By tricking a user into entering their login credentials on a fake login page, an attacker can gain access to the user's account and potentially steal sensitive information.

How to protect against clickjacking attacks on login pages

Fortunately, there are steps you can take to protect yourself against clickjacking attacks:

  1. Use a reputable web browser that has built-in protection against clickjacking attacks.
  2. Never enter your login credentials on a page that looks suspicious or unfamiliar.
  3. Check the URL of the login page to make sure it is legitimate and secure (look for "https" in the URL).
  4. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your accounts to add an extra layer of security.

Conclusion

Clickjacking attacks on login pages are a serious threat, but with the right precautions and awareness, you can protect yourself against them. Always be vigilant when entering sensitive information online, and stay up-to-date on the latest security best practices to stay safe in the ever-evolving world of cybersecurity.
